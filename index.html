Sheet 3: Short References
Rinzin Chodron, daughter of Kangyur Rinpoche 
"A Tibetan doctor, she studied medicine with Kangyur Rinpoche. She is always taking care of people who are not well. She is the incarnation of Kangyur Rinpoche’s mother. In her life, Kangyur Rinpoche’s mother expressed the strong wish to go to India to pay respect to the sacred places. After her passing away, as a way to pay respect to her, Kangyur Rinpoche did a pilgrimage in the sacred places in India and made so many offerings. My sister Rinzin-la was conceived in Tso Pema, so my grandmother was back to the family in a short time after her death. "

Pema Wangyal Rinpoche 

Jetsun Chokyi, daughter of Kangyur Rinpoche 
"Chokyi-la was the one who requested Kangyur Rinpoche to write the Tara Sadhana and also the one who inspired the interruption of the breeding of thousands of sheep and yaks in the family. When they decided to stop the breeding, most of the sheep and yaks started dying naturally. I remember as a child seeing the carcasses of the sheep being given to the dogs."

Pema Wangyal Rinpoche

Jetsun Yangchen, daughter of Kangyur Rinpoche 
"Her previous incarnation was a student of Kangyur Rinpoche who belonged to the family of descendants from Nubchen Sangye Yeshe. After she died, while still wondering in the bardo, Kyabje Kangyur Rinpoche conducted a fire puja in her family house in Nyemo. Nyemo is the land of Vairochana and of Khyungpo Naljor, the founder of the Shangpa Kagyu lineage. That fire puja established the conditions for her to be reborn in Kangyur Rinpoche’s family. At the request of Sangyum Kusho, Jetsun Yangchen made 100,000 tsa tsas of three long life deities dedicated for the long life of Kyabje Dudjom Rinpoche. Sangyum Kusho also requested her to do 100,000 Guru Rinpoche tsa tsas, and at the moment she is completing 100,000 Guru Dorje Drolo tsa tsas. In total, I believe she has made around 700,000 tsa tsas. She is instrumental for the stupa project, constantly preparing the tsa tsas, pills and other ritual articles."

Pema Wangyal Rinpoche
